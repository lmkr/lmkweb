Description

Computer and telecommunication networks, together with their protocols, constitute key building blocks of most modern ICT systems, since they define the infrastructures and the services that make possible the cooperation of users, be they human or machines, through the exchange of information. The engineering of networks, protocols and services supporting today’s advanced use of information technology is a challenging discipline, which requires careful behavioural modelling and validation. This makes networks, protocols and services an important application domain for the use of Petri nets techniques, as well as other models of concurrency. In recent years, Petri net techniques have been successfully applied to the analysis and design of networks, protocols and services for a wide variety of application areas, ranging from high-performance network protocols, to embedded systems and mobile phones, to enterprise applications based on service-oriented architectures, and to web services.

This special issue is dedicated to papers that focus on the practical applications of Petri net-based techniques and technologies, as well as other models of concurrency, to the analysis and design of networks, protocols and services. This special issue will cover a broad range of topics pertinent to the engineering of networks, protocols and services, including (but not limited to):

·         user requirement definition

·         service and protocol specification

·         testing and functional verification

·         performance and dependability evaluation

·         synthesis and code generation

·         assessment of security aspects

Furthermore, we encourage submissions with focus on case studies demonstrating the practical effectiveness of concurrency modelling and associated analysis techniques in the areas above.
